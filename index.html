WCAG and Color Accessibility

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) provide standards to ensure digital content is accessible to people with disabilities, including those with visual impairments. One key aspect of WCAG compliance is color contrast, which ensures text is readable against its background.

1. Color Contrast and Readability

WCAG defines contrast ratios to ensure sufficient distinction between foreground and background colors. The contrast ratio is calculated as:

(L1 + 0.05) / (L2 + 0.05)

where L1 is the relative luminance of the lighter color and L2 is the relative luminance of the darker color.

2. WCAG Contrast Requirements

There are different levels of contrast compliance:

AA Level (Minimum Standard)

  • Normal text: At least 4.5:1 contrast ratio
  • Large text (18pt or 14pt bold): At least 3:1 contrast ratio

AAA Level (Enhanced Standard)

  • Normal text: At least 7:1 contrast ratio
  • Large text: At least 4.5:1 contrast ratio
